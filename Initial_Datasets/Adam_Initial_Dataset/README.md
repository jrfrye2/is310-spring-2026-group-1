# Initial Documentation

## Cultural Materials

My dataset is made up of media headlines and posts discussing Kevin Durant across different points in his career. These headlines come from sports journalism, blogs, and online platforms such as ESPN, Yahoo Sports, Reddit, Twitter/X, and other sports media sources. I constructed the dataset manually by collecting headlines that reflected how Durant was being discussed in the media at the time.

The purpose of the dataset is to explore how media narratives around Kevin Durant have shifted throughout his career. Rather than focusing on statistical performance, the dataset looks at how sports media and online communities frame Durant as a cultural figure. These headlines can reflect storylines about athletes such as rising stardom, controversy, redemption, or legacy debates, throughout their careers.

This type of dataset helps capture how sports culture is shaped not only by performance on the court but also by the stories told by media outlets and online communities.

---

## Computational Tools & Limitations

The dataset was primarily created manually using Google searches and media archives to locate headlines from different periods of Kevin Durant’s career. The data was then manually entered into an Excel sheet.

I used computational tools such as search engines and filters to locate articles and historical coverage. These tools made it easier to find headlines from different years and outlets by using custom time frames.

One limitation of the data is that categorizing themes and tone involves subjective interpretation. Different people may interpret the tone of a headline differently, or classify a narrative under a different theme. Because of this, the dataset reflects interpretive decisions made during the process of structuring the data.
Another limitation is that the dataset only captures a small sample of coverage and may not represent every narrative about Durant that existed at the time.

---

## Decisions on Inclusion / Exclusion

When deciding which headlines to use, I focused on moments that reflected clear media narratives about Kevin Durant. This included headlines related to major career events such as his MVP season, his move to the Golden State Warriors, his championships, his Achilles injury, and later discussions about his legacy.

Headlines that were very repetitive or did not represent a narrative were excluded. The goal was not to capture every headline, but to include examples that demonstrate how the media framed him during different stages of his career.

Another decision involved limiting the dataset to around fifty items so that each entry could be manually examined and categorized. 

---

## Categorization Choices

To structure the dataset, I created several narrative categories that capture common storylines used in sports media.

**Rising Superstar** describes early career coverage that portrays Durant as a young talent expected to become one of the league’s best players.

**Dominant Superstar** reflects headlines focusing on Durant’s performance and achievements, particularly during MVP seasons or strong playoff runs.

**Villain Narrative** refers to media framing that portrays Durant negatively, particularly after his decision to join the Golden State Warriors, which many fans viewed as controversial.

**Social Media Conflict** captures moments where Durant’s online interactions with fans or media became part of the story itself.

**Redemption Arc** reflects moments where Durant’s performance or comeback challenged previous criticism, such as after injury or during strong playoff performances.

Finally, **Legacy Debate** represents later-career narratives that discuss Durant’s place in basketball history, his championships, and comparisons to other players.

These categories were created after reviewing many headlines and noticing recurring patterns in how Durant was discussed across different outlets.

---

## Challenges

One challenge when creating this dataset was determining how to categorize headlines that could fit into multiple narratives. For example, some headlines discussing Durant’s performance also referenced criticism or debates about his career decisions.

To address this, I focused on the primary framing of the headline rather than trying to capture every possible interpretation.

Another challenge involved the diversity of sources. Some headlines came from traditional sports journalism outlets while others came from online communities such as Reddit or social media posts. These platforms sometimes frame athletes very differently, which adds complexity to categorizing tone and narrative themes.

Additionally, locating older headlines required searching across multiple sources and adjusting search terms to find coverage from specific years.

---

## Patterns, Questions, or Tensions That Emerged

While working with the dataset, a clear pattern emerged showing that media narratives around Kevin Durant shift depending on the stage of his career.

Early coverage often framed him as a rising superstar and future face of the league. During his time with the Thunder, many headlines focused on his scoring ability and MVP-level performance.

However, the narrative changed significantly after Durant joined the Golden State Warriors in 2016. Media coverage frequently portrayed him as a villain or criticized his decision to join an already dominant team.

Another interesting pattern is how social media interactions became part of Durant’s public narrative. Unlike earlier eras of athletes, Durant’s online presence and responses to fans often became news stories themselves.

Later coverage increasingly focuses on debates about Durant’s legacy and place in basketball history. Rather than focusing solely on performance, many headlines discuss how his career decisions influence how he will be remembered.

These shifts show how media narratives can shape the public perception of athletes over time.

# Next Steps

To expand the dataset computationally, I plan to scale the collection of headlines using web scraping or automated news searches. This would allow the dataset to grow from approximately 50 items to several hundred headlines covering Kevin Durant across multiple platforms.

Automation could be used to collect headlines from sports media websites and news archives. Natural language processing tools could then help identify keywords related to narrative themes such as criticism, legacy discussions, or performance highlights.

By combining automated collection with manual classification, the dataset could expand while still maintaining interpretive accuracy.
