# Instructor Feedback on Initial Dataset Submission

Hi Adam,

You've done a good job narrowing the scope of the project and defining what you want to collect. Focusing on media coverage of one person across their career is a good way to really dig into how narrative devices are being used in media depictions of an athlete.

Here are a few issues I observed:

1. Categorization and Clarity

First, a few small issues with your dataset and columns:
In the future, please upload your data in csv format so that it is easier to access and read.
In the headline column, you are collecting titles but your description says it includes text. The content you are collecting should be more clear here.

Narrative theme: how are these themes being chosen? Did you read the headlines and decide based on those headlines that you have collected? A clear workflow for how you landed on these themes in particular would be helpful to your reader.

Also, are you choosing the tone categorization based on the headline or the full text of the media coverage.

You write,"...I constructed the dataset manually by collecting headlines that reflected how Durant was being discussed in the media at the time." You should go into more detail about how you chose what headlines to include. Do you mean you collected data that aligns with a pre-established idea of what the narrative around Kevin Durant is? You need to be more descriptive about why you have chosen the categories you chose as well as why each of your data points fits into these categories. For instance, you say that you did not include headlines that "did not represent a narrative." How did you make this determination? Rather than not collecting any data that does not fall into one of the pre-established categories, I would encourage you to use the cases where a headline does not neatly fit the narrative to think about how this complicates your understanding of media representation of Durant's career.

You mention also that you did not include repetitive headlines. However, when thinking about scaling up, repetitive headlines may actually be of use. They could help you get a grasp of which storylines were the most prevalent at any given time in Durant's career or how often statements about him were repeated within the community.

2. Metadata, transparency, and completeness

I do not see any links or full text included in your dataset. You should be including some kind of evidence that the articles/posts/etc. exist for documentation and reproducibility purposes. It looks like you are also collecting only the year for publication. I would also think about adding more exact dates where you can access them since a lot can change about the way a narrative plays out in the media in a year.

Which computational tools are you using to find your data? You say search engines and filters but that tells me very little.

3. Media Tone and Challenges

You are right that the interpretation of tone is subjective. This is where it may be helpful to add contextual information (full-text of the articles explaining the situation, justifications for cases that could have leaned toward positive or negative etc.)

I think the difference between the ways that users in online communities such as Reddit frame the narrative vs. the more traditional media framing is less of a challenge and more of a potentially interesting research question! You may, for example, find that traditional media gives a much cleaner narrative across Durant's career while Redditers tend to focus on other events or emphasize things that traditional media does not. I think that point is definitely worth looking into more.

The shift in emphasis you are finding in your data is interesting, especially his social media presence as a news-worthy topic. Are these typically framed in a positive or negative way? Was this out of the ordinary for the time or is it part of a larger trend toward treating tweets as news?

 In scaling up to automatically collect headlines it is important to keep in mind where you are able to collect from. If you wanted to also collect social media user text you need to consider which social media you can collect from, how it can be obtained, and also how to deal with individual identifying information in the data.

 Good job overall. Please reach out if you have any questions.
