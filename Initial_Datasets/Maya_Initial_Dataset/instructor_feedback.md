Overall, this is a genuinely interesting dataset and I can tell you thought carefully about what variables to include and why. That said, there are some points I want to push back on or flag, both in terms of the submission itself and in terms of earlier feedback that hasn't quite been addressed yet.

**1. The Documentation Needs to Go Deeper on Hard Cases**

The `README.md` explains what each category means very well, but it doesn't walk through how you actually made decisions when an event didn't fit cleanly. That's where the most interesting interpretive work lives. For instance, how did you handle an event that a sports outlet framed negatively but a mainstream celebrity outlet framed positively? Did that push you to rethink your Media Tone categories? How did you decide whether something was "Mixed" versus "Negative"? The column table is genuinely helpful, but I'd also encourage you to be more explicit about which fields involve the most subjective judgment. Media Tone and Event Type in particular are doing a lot of interpretive work, and flagging that more clearly would help readers understand the nature of those fields.

I'd also gently reframe something in the challenges section: you are right to note that subjectivity and platform instability present challenges, but I'd encourage you to see them as features rather than bugs. The subjectivity involved in assessing posts and the instability of platforms are inherently part of the media ecosystem you're studying. Rather than trying to eliminate them, you can embrace them as part of the story you're telling with your data.

I'd also suggest collapsing the Categorization Choices and Decisions on Inclusion/Exclusion sections in a future revision, since they cover overlapping ground. And one small but important clarification: I'm not sure what "constructed the dataset from scratch of events" means? I think you may mean you built the dataset by identifying events that attracted media attention, but it would be worth rephrasing that to make the process clearer.

**2. Improvements to the Dataset**

The dataset currently provides no links to the original sources of the events you've documented, which makes it difficult for others to verify or build on your work. Including links to the specific articles or posts each entry is based on would make the dataset significantly more useful and reproducible. On the same note, saving the dataset as an Excel file rather than a CSV creates unnecessary friction for anyone trying to work with it computationally. CSV is a more universal format that can be read by essentially any software or programming language, and I'd recommend switching.

I also want to flag something worth thinking about as you develop the Media Tone variable further. Online media coverage is increasingly a network effect. Automated news aggregators pull from similar sources and often use similar language, which can create a feedback loop where certain events get more coverage simply because they're already being covered elsewhere, amplifying the tone in the process. That dynamic is itself part of what your dataset is capturing, and thinking carefully about whether your Sources variable can help you trace those feedback loops would be a really interesting direction to pursue.

**3. Advice on The Computational Requirement and Next Steps**

I appreciate that you have initially worked manually and that you are already thinking through how to bring in automation for your next steps. Overall, I think you are on the right track but I have a few suggestions to sharpen it: first, you might try to narrow your focus to one or two platforms, which will make the scraping more manageable and your analysis more focused. Second, before you scale up, it's worth looking at your existing data to see if you can identify patterns in the language of the posts that correspond to your Event Type and Media Tone labels. That exploratory work could form the basis of an automated labeling approach and would also help you assess how consistent your coding has been so far. Next week we will be touching on methods like TF-IDF and topic modeling that can help with this, but you might even just do simple word counts or keyword patterns. You might also consider using an LLM to assist with labeling at scale (something we will be covering in three weeks), which can be a powerful tool for interpretive tasks like these. You could use your existing labeled entries as a training reference and apply that to new data to maintain consistency as you expand.

I think if you continue to approach this thoughtfully as you scale up, you will have a very strong project.
